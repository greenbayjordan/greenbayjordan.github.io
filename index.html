 About

Jordan Packer is a radical urban sociologist, interested in urban equity through food justice, community development, and placemaking.

In the fall of 2020, Jordan will begin her M.S. in Design and Urban Ecologies at the Parsons School of Design. She received her B.A. in sociology and urban studies and planning from UC San Diego in 2018.

Jordanâ€™s previous experiences are community-driven and rooted in sociology. Most recently, she worked for Huerto de la Familia to bolster Latino families food stability through seven organic community garden spaces around Lane County, OR.

From 2017 to 2019, Jordan worked as a staff research associate for Mary Blair-Loy and the Center for Research on Gender in STEM. She studied the subtle gender barriers to the hiring of new faculty in engineering fields. In addition to collecting data, she analyzed the data in SPSS and Stata, produced scholarly texts for publication, mentored and advised undergraduate research assistants.

In her free time, Jordan is an avid ceramist, hiker, and writer.
