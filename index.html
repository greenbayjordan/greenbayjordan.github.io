 About

Jordan Packer received her B.A. in sociology - social inequality and urban studies and planning from UC San Diego in 2018. She is now a staff research associate for the Center for Research on Gender in STEM under Mary Blair-Loy. In this position, Packer studies the subtle gender barriers to the hiring of new academic scientists in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM). She is the primary coder of the video-recordings of candidatesâ€™ formal job talks to determine interactions, such as interruptions. She also analyzes the data in SPSS, produces scholarly texts for publication, advises undergraduate research assistants, and manages the research team's internal and external grants.

Jordan's research interests include education,gender, social networks, and ethnography. Currently, she is studying elite programming, such as undergraduate honors programs, and their role in reproducing inequality. This research's thesis, "The Honors Distinction," culminated spring of 2018, and proceeded to win the UCSD Sociology Department's Outstanding Academic Paper Award.

Fall of 2019, Jordan plans to apply for sociology PhD programs that emphasize education, gender and mixed methods approaches.
