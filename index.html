About


Jordan Packer is a radical sociologist invested in urban equity through community arts and placemaking. She also often analyzes the structures of education and gender.

Jordan received her B.A. in Sociology and Urban Studies and Planning from UC San Diego in 2018.

Jordanâ€™s previous experiences are community-driven and rooted in sociology. Currently, she teaches adult ceramics and youth art classes at Maude Kerns Art Center to build community through creativity. She is also a board member for sustainability and community arts non-profit, MECCA: Materials Exchange Center for Community Arts.

From 2017 to 2019, Jordan worked for the Center for Research on Gender in STEM, studying the subtle gender barriers to the hiring of faculty in engineering fields. In addition to collecting and analyzing data, she produced scholarly texts and advised undergraduate research assistants.

In her free time, Jordan is an avid ceramicist and hiker.
