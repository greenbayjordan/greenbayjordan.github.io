About

Jordan Packer received her B.A. in sociology - social inequality and urban studies and planning from UC San Diego. Now, she is a staff research associate for the Center for Research on Gender in STEMM at UC San Diego. In this position, Packer analyzes job talks for STEM tenure-track applicants through coding video recordings and provides support for the production of scholarly texts.

Packer's research interests include sociology of education, gender, and social networks. Currently, Packer is researching undergraduate honors programs and their role in reproducing inequality. This research's preliminary thesis, "The Honors Distinction," culminated spring of 2018, and proceeded to win the UCSD Sociology Department's Outstanding Academic Paper Award. Packer is now revising the work for submission to Sociology of Education.

Next fall, Packer plans to pursue a PhD in sociology after her staff research associate fellowship.
